Research

My main interests are the spectral analysis and the low-rank factorization of matrices.
I have worked on matrix sequences coming from the discretization of linear PDE, on structured matrices used for quantum communications, on random matrix polynomial with growing size and/or degree, on spectral properties of matrices with functional entries, on perturbation of Hermitian matrices, and on iterative methods in signal processing.
Moreover, I have worked in community detection, on clustering problems for directed graphs, and on machine learning techniques aimed to identify symplex structures through the lens of low-rank factorization methods, with a focus on nonnegative matrix factorization.
More recently, I am interested on symmetric cones coming from Jordan algebras and on indefinite quadratic problems with conic restrictions.

Supported by the ERC consolidator grant no 101085607 of the European Union (Sep 2023 - Jun 2026)
Project: Beyond Low-Rank Factorizations (eLinoR)
